7 Wonders of the World

Christ the Redeemer

Christ the Redeemer, a colossal statue of Jesus, stands atop Mount Corcovado in Rio de Janeiro. It was designed by Heitor da Silva Costa, Carlos Oswald, and Paul Landowsk.

Colosseum

The Colosseum in Rome was built in the 1st century by the emperor Vespasian. It could hold 50,000 spectators who watched events like gladiator fights.

Great Wall of China

The Great Wall of China, one of the world's largest construction projects, is 5,500 miles long. It started in the 7th century BCE and continued for two millennia.

Chichén Itzá

Chichén Itzá is a Mayan city on the Yucatán Peninsula in Mexico, flourishing in the 9th and 10th centuries CE. Among the most notable is the stepped pyramid El Castillo.

Petra

Petra, an ancient city in Jordan, is located in a remote valley nestled among sandstone mountains and cliffs. It was an important trade center, especially for spices.

Machu Picchu

Machu Picchu, an Incan site near Cuzco, Peru, was discovered in 1911 by Hiram Bingham. It was an important stronghold used during the 16th-century rebellion against Spanish rule.

Taj Mahal

The Taj Mahal, a mausoleum complex in Agra, India, is regarded as one of the world’s most iconic monuments. It was built by Emperor Shah Jahan to honor his wife Mumtaz Mahal.
